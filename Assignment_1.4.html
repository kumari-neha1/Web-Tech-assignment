difference between &It;ol> and &It;ul> tags

both &It;ol> and&It;ul> tags are used to create lists in HTML, but they serve different purposes:

  • &It;ol> (Ordered lists) - Creates a list with items that are numbered or ordered sequence each list item is automatically numbered by the browser.
  • &It;ul> (unorderd list) - Creates a list with items that are bulleted. the order of items is not important.

Basic Structure of &It;ol> (Ordered List):/h2> 
    &It;ol>
         &It;li>First Item&It;/li>
         &It;li> Second Item&It;/li>
         &It;li>Third Item&It;/li>
    &It;/ol>

Basic Structure of &It;ul> (Unordered List): 

    &It;ul>
        &It;li>Laptop&It;/li>
        &It;li>Computer&It;/li>
        &It;li>Mobile&It;/li>
    &It;/ul>

Explanation of Usage:

  • use &It;ol> when the sequence of items matters, such as steps in a process, ranking,
  • use &It;ul> when the order does not matter, such as a list of ingredients, features,
